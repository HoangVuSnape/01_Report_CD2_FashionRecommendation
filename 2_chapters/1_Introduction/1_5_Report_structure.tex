\section{Report structure}

The remainder of this report is organized into four chapters:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Chapter 2 (Background)} reviews the theoretical foundations of the study, covering collaborative, content-based, and hybrid recommender systems, graph-based collaborative filtering and the LightGCN propagation scheme, multimodal feature extraction and fusion strategies for fashion items, and the top-$N$ ranking metrics used for evaluation.
    \item \textbf{Chapter 3 (Methods and Models)} presents the proposed data processing pipeline, the LightGCN collaborative-filtering backbone together with the three multimodal graph architectures evaluated in this study (CombiGCN adapted, BM3, and FREEDOM), and the unified multimodal integration design that maps heterogeneous visual and textual features into a shared latent space.
    \item \textbf{Chapter 4 (Experiments)} describes the Vibrent Clothes Rental (VCR) dataset and preprocessing, the experimental setup and research questions, and reports the empirical results and comparative analysis across the $24$ model configurations.
    \item \textbf{Chapter 5 (Conclusion)} summarizes the main findings, discusses the limitations of the study, and outlines directions for future work.
\end{itemize}
