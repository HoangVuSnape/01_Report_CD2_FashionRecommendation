\chapter*{INTEGRATING MULTIMODAL REPRESENTATIONS INTO GRAPH-BASED FASHION RECOMMENDER SYSTEMS\\ABSTRACT}

To combat severe interaction sparsity in fashion recommendation, this work integrates visual and textual representations into Graph Neural Network (GNN) recommenders. Built on a LightGCN backbone, we benchmark three multimodal models (CombiGCN, BM3, and FREEDOM) across twenty-four configurations---varying visual encoders (MobileNetV2 vs. CLIP), text features, and fusion mechanisms (late fusion vs. attention gating)---on a highly sparse subset of the Vibrent Clothes Rental (VCR) dataset (9,455 interactions, 553 users, 2,194 items, 99.22\% sparsity).

Our empirical analysis reveals three key insights. First, MobileNetV2 performs comparably to or better than CLIP when configured optimally (e.g., boosting BM3's NDCG@10 by 30.8\% from 0.0142 to 0.0186), showing that local texture details from CNNs are highly effective for styling. Second, simple late fusion is more robust than trainable attention gating, which overfits on sparse data and causes a 46\% performance drop in BM3. Third, model ranking varies with retrieval depth: BM3's self-supervised alignment excels at $K \ge 5$ (achieving the highest NDCG@5 of 0.0162 and NDCG@10 of 0.0186), whereas CombiGCN is highly competitive for top-1 recommendations (tying at NDCG@1 of 0.0127). These findings demonstrate that multimodal features effectively mitigate data sparsity and provide practical guidelines for graph-based fashion recommendation.
